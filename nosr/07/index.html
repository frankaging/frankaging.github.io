Claude read my website and analysed me critically. Here is what happened.

prompt

Read every HTML file and linked page in this personal website repository. This is not my website; I cloned it from someone else, and I want to understand this person's personality. Based on the content, infer this person's possible MBTI type and summarize their personality in one paragraph. Then give your impressions from four perspectives: if you wanted to date him, if you wanted to be friends with him, if you wanted to hire him, and if you wanted to work with him as a colleague. In each case, focus on both personality strengths and weaknesses. Be critical, direct, and concise.

The evidence, it claimed, was consistent across every layer of the site. Writing personal essays labeled "no one should read" points to introverted Fi processing. Research focused on understanding rather than building. The pull toward acceptance and impermanence — Su Shi, the Heart Sutra, the storm and the clearing. Noticing what others miss: a woman checking her pocket mid-kiss, ink spreading in clean water, and assigning these moments profound weight. Quiet defiance of convention: the punk nostalgia, the lowercase. Deep private values, wide-ranging perception, low need for external validation. INFP.

Then a summary: Zen is a quiet, philosophically serious person who processes the world through careful observation and internal meaning-making. Genuinely cross-disciplinary — aerospace, cloud, NLP — not as a resume flex but because curiosity pulls him across fields. Drawn to impermanence and acceptance to an unusual degree for someone in his twenties, suggesting he has either gone through something difficult or done serious inner work, possibly both. He writes beautifully but only for himself. He prefers process over outcome, understanding over achievement, authenticity over performance. The risk is that this rich inner life is largely self-contained — he is more likely to write a private essay about a feeling than to tell you about it.

Dating

He notices the small things — the kind of partner who remembers the exact light at a particular moment. He is not performing; what you see is what you get. His philosophical depth means he will actually engage with your inner life, not just your surface. He is romantic in an understated, ritualistic way. That said, he is emotionally inward to a fault. The NOSR concept is telling: his deepest processing happens alone, not in relationship. He may intellectualize feelings rather than share them. His attachment to childhood memories and nostalgia can be beautiful but also signals difficulty being fully present. Expect some catching up — a fresh PhD who likely deprioritized relationships for years.

Friendship

Excellent conversationalist on topics that matter to him — AI, Chinese philosophy, physics, aesthetics. Not performative; you will not get small talk. Loyal once trust is established. He will introduce you to Su Shi and make you care about it. Hard to maintain contact with, though. His social energy seems finite and self-directed. The NOSR section feels less like wanting to connect with someone and more like wanting a record for his future self. He likely has few close friends and keeps them at some emotional distance. Do not expect him to reach out first.

Hiring

Genuine original thinker. ReFT, pyvene, and Boundless DAS are real contributions, not incremental work. He is willing to critique his own research area — representation steering lags behind simple prompting baselines — which is rare honesty. Cross-domain background gives unusual perspective. He builds tools other researchers actually use. On the other hand, he has a very specific thesis about what matters and will likely disengage from work outside that lane. He may prioritize understanding over shipping, which is fine for research but friction in a product organization. He will not hustle for visibility.

Colleague

Intellectually honest, does not oversell, builds shared infrastructure. His acceptance of impermanence means he handles ambiguity without drama. He will not play politics. The same acceptance, taken too far, can read as passivity — he may disengage from team friction rather than resolving it. Emotionally opaque: you will not easily know when something is wrong. He processes privately, so misalignments may go unstated until they grow. In a fast-moving team that needs vocal contributors in brainstorms, he will be underrepresented.

I have read all the comments above and corrected my personality shortcomings wholeheartedly.
